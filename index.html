What is NSTI?

Down into the details

    Language is a fundamental aspect of human communication, serving as a means of expressing thoughts, emotions, and beliefs. One of the earliest examples of language is the Sumerian language of ancient Sumer. Since then, the ways we communicate, express, and interpret ideas have transformed substantially with the advent of digital communication and the blending of cultural influences. Language has a profound impact on shaping interpersonal dynamics, identity, and even self-perception. In today’s rapidly evolving world, communication patterns change more swiftly than they may seem. Notable examples, such as the viral song “Skibidi Toilet” by Alexey Gerasimov, have contributed to language evolution.

    Drawing on frameworks like the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI), which categorises personality traits to help understand behavioural tendencies. We aim to uncover patterns that reflect modern psychological landscapes, from emotional expression to adaptability in varied social contexts. The Nai-Sivakorn Type Indicator is a revised type indicator adapted to the modern knowledge and expressions perceived by Gen Z teenagers. Tailored to reflect emerging social dynamics and linguistic shifts, the NSTI captures the intricacies of modern communication and self-expression, incorporating elements unique to the current generation. Concepts like “Rizz,” a term that speaks to the charm or social charisma individuals wield in digital and social interactions, and privately held yet prevalent expressions of biases and modernised racial perceptions, illustrate how identity and social boundaries are navigated among youth today.

    The NSTI serves as a lens to decode these updated modes of interaction, examining how linguistic innovation and evolving social perceptions contribute to distinct personality types and behavioural standards within Generation Alpha. In doing so, the NSTI provides a contemporary approach to understanding the interplay between language and behaviour, shedding light on the emerging psychological profiles of a generation shaping tomorrow’s cultural norms.
